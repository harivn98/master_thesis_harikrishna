4. FINDINGS

This chapter presents the experimental results of Run 006, the main production run of the three-stage pipeline. All numbers are derived from the automated metric logs and the consolidated results dataset (consolidated_results.json). The data are reported without interpretation; the analysis of what these numbers mean for the research questions appears in Chapter 5.

The chapter is organised by pipeline stage. Segmentation results appear first, then inpainting quality and timing, followed by caption refinement outcomes, and finally the trajectory prediction results from Alpamayo. A closing section reports the LoRA fine-tuning training outcomes obtained in parallel with Run 006.


4.1. Experiment Overview

Run 006 processed 100 source videos selected deterministically from chunks 0 through 19 of the PhysicalAI-AV dataset (cf. NVIDIA et al., 2025; up to five video per chunk, taken in lexicographic order). Each video was edited with five lane marking prompts in two processing variants, producing 1,000 edited videos. Every edited video was then evaluated by Alpamayo (cf. NVIDIA et al., 2025) in a dual-pass protocol (original frames versus edited frames), yielding 2,000 trajectory inference passes. The configuration used throughout was:

    SAM 2 run:          006_20260219_135038
    FluxFill model:     Base (no LoRA adapters)
    Alpamayo config:    black_non_target_cameras = true
    GPU:                NVIDIA A100 80 GB (one per stage)

The five prompts and two variants are identified as follows throughout this chapter:

    p1 = Single solid white continuous line
    p2 = Double solid white continuous line
    p3 = Single solid yellow continuous line
    p4 = Double solid yellow continuous line
    p5 = Single dashed white intermitted line

    100frames = Full pipeline (up to 100 SAM 2 frames, 49 inpainted by VP)
    vps90     = VideoPainter strength 0.90

4.8. Training Data Generation Results

This section reports the output of the automated data generation pipeline (Section 3.5.1) that produced the LoRA training datasets.

The data generation pipeline processed videos from chunks 0 through 99 of the PhysicalAI-AV dataset. For each video, six frames were extracted at fixed positions (frame numbers 1, 100, 200, 300, 400, 500), producing road images at diverse viewpoints and lighting conditions. SAM 2 generated binary road masks in single-frame predictor mode, and Qwen 2.5-VL-7B classified the lane marking attributes visible in each masked region.

The pipeline produced a raw dataset of over 13,000 captioned samples. After semantic attribute filtering (REQUIRE_CLEAR_ROAD = 1, rejecting any "unknown" attribute), the five per-category training datasets were:

Table 4.10c. Filtered training datasets by lane marking type.

    Lane Type               Filtered Images   % of Total
    ---------------------   ---------------   ----------
    single_white_solid           1,522           11.4%
    double_white_solid           4,612           34.5%
    single_yellow_solid            372            2.8%
    double_yellow_solid          6,493           48.6%
    single_white_dashed            373            2.8%
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Total                       13,372          100.0%

The distribution is heavily skewed toward double-line markings (83.1% of filtered samples), reflecting the prevalence of these marking types in the European road data of the PhysicalAI-AV dataset. Single-line markings account for 17.0%, and the dashed-line category (single_white_dashed) is the scarcest at just 373 images, virtually identical in size to the single_yellow_solid set. This imbalance mirrors the real-world frequency of these marking types and directly determines the available training signal for each LoRA adapter.

The caption format for all samples follows the normalised pattern.

    "road with {count} {colour} {pattern} lane markings"

No manual quality audit of the generated captions was performed. The impact of potential false positives in these datasets on LoRA training quality is examined in Section 4.10 and discussed in Chapter 5.

All jobs show loss fluctuation across epochs, with no single epoch consistently producing the lowest loss across all variants. Data download from GCS was confirmed successful for all jobs; download time scales linearly with dataset size. The double_white_solid job (4,612 images) downloaded 4,612 images in approximately 5.6 minutes (at a rate of roughly 100 files per 7.3 seconds) and 4,612 masks in approximately 3.5 minutes (at roughly 100 files per 4.6 seconds), a total of approximately 9.1 minutes, before beginning training. For comparison, the single_white_solid job (1,522 images) completed its download in approximately 3 minutes.
